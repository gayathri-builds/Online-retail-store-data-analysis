## 🧭 Overview

This repository documents my journey through the **Tata Group x Forage Virtual Job Simulation** on Data Visualization and Business Intelligence. More than a technical exercise, this experience challenged me to think like a **business analyst** — to stop building charts and start solving problems.

The simulation pushed me to ask three critical questions before touching any tool:

- ❓ **What does the business actually need?**
- ❓ **Who is looking at this data, and what decision are they trying to make?**
- ❓ **Am I showing the right insight — or just showing data?**

---

## 🗂️ Simulation Structure

The program was structured across **4 progressive tasks**, each building on the last:

---

### ✅ Task 1 — Framing the Business Scenario

Before building anything, I had to understand what the **CEO and CMO** actually needed to know.

**Key Takeaway:**
> The most important step in any analysis isn't the tool — it's asking the right question.

This task grounded the entire simulation in **business-first thinking** rather than data-first thinking.

---

### ✅ Task 2 — Choosing the Right Visuals

Selecting chart types isn't aesthetic — it's strategic. The wrong visual can **mislead even the most accurate data**.

**Key Takeaway:**
> Visualization is a communication decision, not a design decision.

---

### ✅ Task 3 — Creating Effective Visuals (Power BI)

This is where the data came to life. I built **4 dashboards** in Power BI:

| Dashboard | Key Insight |
|---|---|
| 📅 **Monthly Revenue Trend (2011)** | Revenue peaked at **$1.08M in November** (seasonal demand); lowest in February at **$0.40M** |
| 🌍 **Top 10 Countries by Revenue & Quantity** | **Netherlands** topped with **$270K** in revenue — revealing strong markets beyond the UK |
| 👤 **Top 10 Customers by Revenue** | Customer **#16446** generated **$337K** in revenue; Pareto-style view identified highest-value relationships |
| 🌐 **Global Demand Distribution** | Netherlands led international demand at **193K units**, followed by EIRE (135K) and Germany (113K) |

---

### ✅ Task 4 — Communicating Insights and Analysis

The final task transformed numbers into a **story that a business leader could act on**.

**Key Takeaway:**
> Data without communication is just noise. The ability to translate analysis into decisions is the most valuable skill in any data role.
